618.04 POISONING ANIMALS.

     (a)     No person shall maliciously, or willfully and without the consent of the owner, administer poison, except a licensed veterinarian acting in such capacity, to a farm animal, dog, cat, poultry or other domestic animal that is the property of another; and no person shall, willfully and without the consent of the owner, place any poisoned food where it may be easily found and eaten by any of such animals, either upon his own lands or the lands of another.  This section does not apply to trespassing animals as set forth in Ohio R.C. 959.04.  (ORC 959.03)
 

     (b)     Whoever violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor of the fourth degree.  (ORC 959.99(C))
